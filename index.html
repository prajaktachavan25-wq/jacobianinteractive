Understanding the Jacobian

In multivariable calculus, the Jacobian Matrix is the best linear approximation of a function near a specific point. Think of it as the "multi-dimensional slope."

The Core Steps
  1. Identify Functions: Separate your equations into $u(x,y)$ and $v(x,y)$.
  2. Partial Differentiation: Differentiate each function with respect to every variable separately.
  3. Arrange in Matrix: Place $u$'s derivatives in Row 1 and $v$'s in Row 2.
  4. Find Determinant: Calculate $ad - bc$ to find the Jacobian Determinant.
J = (∂u/∂x)(∂v/∂y) - (∂u/∂y)(∂v/∂x)
