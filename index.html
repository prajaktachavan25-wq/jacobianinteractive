Mastering Jacobian Steps

Step 1: The Function Setup

Identify your functions $u(x,y)$ and $v(x,y)$ from the problem statement.

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

Differentiate each function. When finding $\frac{\partial u}{\partial x}$, treat $y$ as a fixed constant.

Step 3: The Inverse Property

Remember that $JJ' = 1$. This means the Jacobian of $(u,v)$ with respect to $(x,y)$ is the reciprocal of the inverse.

What is JJ' ?

(Click to reveal the theorem)

JJ' = 1

The product of a Jacobian and its inverse Jacobian is always equal to 1.
